Case Study 04 — AI Search Visibility (GEO/AEO) at Perceptive Analytics

First-Mover AI Search: Brand Citations in ChatGPT, Gemini & Perplexity Before Competitors Knew It Mattered

Building a durable competitive moat through LLM reverse-engineering, structured content clusters, and entity authority.

Insight
B2B buyers discover vendors through AI assistants. First-mover brand citation = durable competitive moat.
Challenge
No existing framework for LLM visibility. Required building a self-reinforcing system — not a one-time campaign.
Approach
Designed a cyclic engine: Research → Create → Publish → Optimise → Measure → Repeat. The system compounds over time.
The Cyclic System Architecture
Cycle 01
LLM Reverse-Engineering & Prompt Research
Continuously map the top prompts B2B buyers use for target keywords. Reverse-engineer how ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity generate vendor citations — identifying evolving data signals, content structures, and source-selection patterns. This research feeds directly into the content cycle.
Cycle 02
Cyclic Content Creation & Publication
Ongoing production of AEO-optimised content aligned to AI response patterns: Whitepapers · C-Suite Briefs · Case Studies · Benchmarks · FAQ Schema. Each piece is structured for LLM consumption, interlinked into authority clusters, and published on a continuous cadence — not a one-time batch.
Cycle 03
Technical & Non-Technical Optimisation
Technical: FAQ schema markup, structured data, page speed, canonical tags, internal linking architecture. Non-Technical: Entity building across business directories, author authority profiles, About Us credibility signals, off-page presence in national publications and media pitches. Both layers compound with each cycle.
Cycle 04
Measurement & Feedback Loop
Weekly LLM citation tracking across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity for priority keywords. Citation frequency, position, and context feed back into the research cycle — creating a self-reinforcing loop where each iteration improves the next.

90-Day GTM Blueprint

Scaling a Series-B Developer Tools SaaS from $2M to $5M ARR: My Playbook

A detailed go-to-market strategy demonstrating how I'd architect sales-marketing alignment, demand infrastructure, and revenue operations at a high-growth startup.

Company
DevStack (fictional) — CI/CD automation platform for engineering teams
Stage
Series B · $18M raised · 2-person marketing team
Current State
$2M ARR · 500 customers · PLG motion only · No enterprise sales playbook
Board Goal
$5M ARR within 12 months · Build repeatable enterprise sales motion
Sales-Marketing Alignment Model

Revenue happens when sales and marketing operate as one system, not two departments. Here's how I'd structure the handshake:

🎯 Shared Pipeline KPIs
Both teams measured on net-new pipeline dollars — not MQLs, not activities. Marketing commits to pipeline contribution targets. Sales commits to SLA on lead follow-up. Shared weekly dashboard in HubSpot with real-time pipeline visibility.
🤝 Lead SLA & Handoff Protocol
Marketing delivers intent-qualified accounts with account dossiers (6sense engagement score, tech stack, key contacts, recent content interactions). Sales responds within 4 hours. Every handoff includes a "why this account, why now" rationale — same system I built at Perceptive.
🔄 Closed-Loop CRM Feedback
Every won/lost deal feeds back to marketing via custom HubSpot properties: objection reasons, competitor mentions, content that influenced the deal. This data directly informs messaging, content, and channel allocation. Weekly deal review between marketing and sales leads.
🏗️ Joint Account Planning (Top 50)
Marketing and sales co-select the top 50 target accounts each quarter. Marketing runs multi-channel surround (LinkedIn, Google Ads, email, AEO content). Sales runs personalised outreach. Monthly account-level pipeline review to rebalance targeting.
90-Day Execution Sprint
D1
Days 1–30: Foundation & ICP Lock
ICP Definition: Use 6sense to identify companies actively evaluating CI/CD tools by buying stage. Map buying committee: VP Engineering (sponsor), DevOps Lead (champion), CTO (approver). Infrastructure: HubSpot → Salesforce integration, lead scoring model, attribution framework. Sales Enablement: Account dossier template, intent alert automation via 6sense, Calendly booking for qualified accounts. Baseline Measurement: Current CAC ($X), LTV ($Y), pipeline velocity, conversion rates by stage.
→ Deliverables: ICP doc, account list (200 Tier-A), integrated CRM, attribution model
D2
Days 31–60: Engine Build & Channel Launch
ABM: Activate top 200 enterprise targets via 6sense intent data. Multi-channel surround: LinkedIn Ads targeting VP Eng/CTO + Google Ads SKAGs for "CI/CD automation" + "DevOps pipeline tools." Content & AEO: Publish 10 AEO-optimised articles targeting AI search queries developers use. Build entity authority via Stack Overflow, GitHub Discussions, dev community content. PLG → Sales Handoff: Product usage triggers (e.g., team size > 5, CI pipeline > 100 runs/day) → auto-MQL → sales alert via HubSpot workflow + 6sense engagement score. Nurture: 5-touchpoint email sequence segmented by persona and intent stage.
→ Deliverables: 3 paid campaigns live, 10 AEO articles published, PLG handoff triggers active
D3
Days 61–90: Optimise, Prove, Scale
CRO Sprint: Deploy Microsoft Clarity across all landing pages. Heatmap analysis, session recordings, form optimisation. A/B test pricing page, demo booking flow, and case study layouts. Attribution & Reporting: Unified dashboard (GA4 + HubSpot + Salesforce + LinkedIn + Google Ads). First pipeline metrics review with sales leadership. CAC by channel, pipeline velocity by source, MQL→SQL conversion by persona. Sales Feedback: First closed-loop review. Refine messaging based on objection data. Adjust channel mix based on pipeline attribution. Scale Decision: Double down on top 2 performing channels. Expand ABM list if Tier-A conversion holds.
→ Deliverables: CRO report, first pipeline attribution review, scale/kill decisions documented
